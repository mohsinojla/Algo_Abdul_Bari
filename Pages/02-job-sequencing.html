🟠 Greedy Algorithm #2
📋

Job Sequencing with Deadlines

Schedule jobs greedily to maximize total profit before their deadlines.

▶ Watch Abdul Bari on YouTube
📖 Concept Overview

You have n jobs, each taking exactly 1 time unit. Each job has a profit and a deadline. You can do at most one job per time slot.

Goal: Select a subset of jobs and schedule them to maximize total profit without missing any deadline.

Greedy Strategy: Sort jobs by profit in descending order. For each job (most profitable first), try to schedule it in the latest available slot ≤ its deadline.

Sort by Profit ↓ → For each job, assign to latest free slot ≤ deadline
💡 Why latest available slot? Scheduling a job as late as possible leaves earlier slots free for other high-profit jobs.
🔑 Key Points
1. All jobs take exactly 1 unit of time.
2. Sort jobs by profit in descending order.
3. Maintain a slot array of size = max deadline. All slots initially free.
4. For each job: find the latest free slot ≤ deadline and assign it.
5. If no free slot available before deadline → skip the job.
6. Time Complexity: O(n²) for simple approach, O(n log n) with Union-Find.
7. Output: Selected jobs + their order + total profit.
OperationComplexity
SortingO(n log n)
Scheduling (naive)O(n²)
Overall (exam)O(n²)
